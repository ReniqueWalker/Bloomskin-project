Oily

Oily skin is a problem that occurs when oversized sebaceous glands produce excessive amounts of sebum giving the appearance of shiny skin which can feel greasy to the touch.

Dry

Some of the common characteristics of dry skin include skin appearing rough, flaky or scaly and becoming easily irritable or itchy. Dry skin can also look dull or feel tight, which can highlight the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

Normal

All skin is normal, but if your skin is well-balanced and doesn’t feel dry or oily, your skincare type is classed as Normal. This means you probably don’t suffer from breakouts or flakiness and your skin is smooth with pores that look refined. Normal skin also feels quite elastic and comfortable.

Combination

Combination skin is when your skin shows characteristics of different skin types. You might have an oily T-zone and dry or normal cheeks, for example. If your skin is showing traits of both dryness and oiliness, then there’s a good chance you have combination skin.
